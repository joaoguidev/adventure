Lens of Curiosity

Back

Gone home is a perfect game when it comes to exciting the player's curiosity. The game starts off by having the player come home to find that none of their family members are home. So the player is instantly off the bat intrigued to look around the house for clues to see where their family is. This isn't all though. That game is built in the mind of exploration. So every drawer and cupboard and so on can be opened and explored for clues.

This is terrific because as the player keeps opening drawers and such and finding more clues the more they learn about their missing family and some past history intriguing them to keep exploring or as some would consider snoop. The player gets clues and answers to any previous questions they might have had. What keeps the player motivated to keep exploring that even though every piece of evidence answers a previous question it also sparks up new and more interesting questions. You start the game wondering where your family is and you end up asking more questions than you would have ever expected.

It feels enjoyable to undiscover these clues because you get to learn about the tragic history all your family members have gone through or are going through. This game is very lore-heavy so the more you discover the more interesting the story gets. The gameplay on average is only about 4 hours so in one short burst, most people will play this game in one playthrough because it's like reading a book that you can interact with. It rewards the player with heavy lore as they keep discovering clues as they go. This game made walking sims actually enjoyable.
