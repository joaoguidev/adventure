How Impactful was Gone Home?

Gone Home was one of the first meldings of walking simulators and adventure games. At the time walking simulators were a new genre pioneered by Dear Esther and were mainly about walking from one place to another as the story was told to you without much to do other than follow the linear path. Adventure games were a much older genre based around exploring and story sections with constant breaks for non-contextual puzzles. Gone Home, when deciding to combine the genres, took the best parts of both and managed to create a game that would revolutionize the indie game sphere and create it’s very own sub-genre of story-focused adventure games.

It managed to inspire many other indie games such as What Remains of Edith Finch, Firewatch, and Virginia. It’s excellently made house that managed to tell the story of those living in it also inspired sections of Uncharted 4, which contains two sections where you walk through the Drake’s households and learn what they have been up to since you last saw them. Gone Home was a landmark game in the adventure game and Walking Simulator genres and may stand the test of time as one of the most influential games when it comes to environmental storytelling.
