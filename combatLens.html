Lens of Secrets

Back

Gone Home is a very deep game if given the chance, although the player could beat the game in an hour at the end of it they would have missed what makes the game shine, it’s hidden lore. The lore and backstories in this game are easy to pass by and can be difficult to discover everything on the players first try of the game, from the mother thinking of another man and the father being a failed writer at first look into these two characters the player would never find that out. However, by digging through hidden articles and finding secret object around the house the player can slowly piece together this lore about them, not only does this make the player feel more for these two characters they never meet by it ties into what we learn about them in the story and how their personality affects the ones around them, without these hidden lore/secrets the games story might seem dull or not very detailed.

Not all the lore is easy for the player to discover some things like the father’s connection to the mansions previous owner is completely missed if the player doesn’t take their time and try hard at this game, each secret can quickly change how the player feels from happy to suddenly shocked and sad, at the end the player might be anxious to go to the attic scared of what they will find while also be worried about the father and the financial standing of the family. The secrets in Gone Home are very detailed and a definite plus for this game although the storyline about Sam and where everyone is, is important. The secrets are what flesh out this game and give it a deeper connection to the player as they explore the old mansion.
